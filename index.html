Welcome

I hope this gives you some new ideas on how to protect our environment. Thank you for reading!

Diet

Eat more plants! Animal agriculture accounts for more greenhouse gas emissions than transportation!

Consider reducing the amount of animal products you consume, especially beef. Learn some vegetarian recipes or choose vegetarian meals at restaurants. Make simple subsitutions, like choosing plant-based milks.

Healthy Habits

Protecting the environment can benefit your health.

  • Cook! This reduces the need for food packaging.
  • Walk or bike, when you can.
  • Clean your home and your body with natural ingredients.

Reducing Waste

Reduce the amount of garbage that you contribute to landfills.

  • Don't let food spoil. Plan your meals and eat what you have.
  • Save the water you use to rinse your produce and use it to water your plants.
  • Reuse containers and grocery bags.
  • Bring your own bags when you shop.
  • Use rags instead of paper towls.
  • If you eat out, refuse straws and paper napkins. Bring your own food containers for leftovers.

Conscious Shopping

Learn about where products come from and how they are made. Research companies to learn about their business practices, and find out what charities they support.

When possible, buy local, organic produce. Avoid single-use products.

Don't shop beause you're bored. Buy quality items that will last. Buy used or borrow when you can.

Composting

Food products decompose slowly inside of plastic trash bags. Composting facilitates food decomposition, and, when added to soil, gives plants needed nutrients.

If you have space, begin composting. If you live in a city, look for communal composting programs in your area.
